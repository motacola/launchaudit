Manual website QA misses things.

Redesigns, migrations, and content launches often end with teams comparing spreadsheets to live pages by hand. That’s slow, inconsistent, and exactly where missing URLs and mismatched pages slip through.

Launches ship with holes

Expected pages get skipped, renamed, or quietly dropped during implementation.

QA is spreadsheet-heavy

Teams still spend hours checking page maps, links, statuses, and metadata manually.

Clients notice late

Missing and broken pages often show up after handoff, launch, or campaign traffic starts.

What the audit catches

LaunchAudit compares your live site against the structure you expected to ship and returns a clean discrepancy report.

Missing pages

Pages that should exist but never made it live.

Broken pages

Expected URLs that exist but return bad responses or errors.

Title mismatches

Pages whose metadata or headings don’t line up with the expected page intent.

Content gaps

Signals that key expected content isn’t represented cleanly on the live site.

Review-required pages

Ambiguous or low-confidence matches that need a human check before sign-off.

Structure drift

Places where the content map and the live implementation no longer align.
